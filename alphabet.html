Alpahbet Soup

Brief:

create a 15 sec video of the alphabet suitable for small children (3-8 years old)

Solution:

  • chose a thick font to make it easier for young children to process the shapes in short amount of time
  • used capitalized letters because they are more familiar to young children
  • to make more entertaining for children, added personalities to letters (M & N) and made little "reveal" letters (O + P=Q)/(v + v=w)

Proccess:

Brainstorm: exploring the brief and indentifying the general theme and some key ideas and concept

Storyboard: mapping out the transitions and big movements // still working on mapping to consistent timing

Font Choice: I was looking for playful, legible, and familiar letters
Nove and Bubblegum Pop have too many hand drawn qualities to a point where I was concerned that it wouldn't be recognizable to younger children. This wouldn't be functional, especially since the time restraint meant that there wouldn't be enough exposure for children to adjust and decipher.
Tomark Display is bold and playful in its shape whilst still similar enough to block letters that are usually used in childrens' content.

zooming in and lowering/increasing opacity to add depth by simulating camera movement

creating fun interaction between letters to add personality to the letters

making the letter shape more fun by creating it with two other letters
